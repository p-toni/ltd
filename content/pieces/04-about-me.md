### How I work
- Clarity first. Translate fuzzy goals into testable hypotheses and guardrails; pick the smallest slice that proves value.
- Observable workflows. Traceable inputs → steps → outputs; stochastic exploration to widen options, deterministic checks where decisions matter.
- Human-in-the-loop by design.

Update (2026-02-08): Human-in-the-loop (HITL) machine learning is a collaborative approach that integrates human input into AI systems, enhancing their effectiveness and reliability. This methodology allows for continuous feedback and improvement, ensuring that human judgment and expertise guide the AI's decision-making processes. By incorporating human oversight, HITL not only improves the quality of outcomes but also aligns AI systems more closely with user needs and ethical standards. This approach supports the principles of clarity, integrity, and observable workflows, ultimately leading to more robust and trustworthy AI applications. (Source: https://cloud.google.com/discover/human-in-the-loop)

Update (2026-02-08): Recent insights highlight that AI in design workflows significantly benefits from human guidance. As emphasized in the article 'How to Supercharge Your Design Workflow with AI,' integrating human oversight is crucial for effectively applying AI technologies. This approach not only enhances the design process but also ensures that the outcomes align with human standards and expectations. By maintaining a balance between human intuition and AI capabilities, teams can achieve more innovative and reliable results. This reinforces the importance of the human-in-the-loop design philosophy in my work. (Source: https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/how-to-supercharge-your-design-workflow-with-ai)

 Keep people as goal-setters and editors; use AI for proposals, drafts, counter-examples, and stress tests.
- Instrumentation over intuition. Ship with evaluators, logs, and quality checks; iterate on real signals, not vibes.
- Integrity is a feature. Reliability, safety, explainability, and security are product requirements—not add-ons.

### What I do
